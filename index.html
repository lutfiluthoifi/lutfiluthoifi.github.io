Delivery Delay Risk & Profitability Analysis
57.3% deliveries late → ~$914K profit at risk driven by SLA mismatch

Business Problem:
High late delivery rate (57.3%) impacting customer satisfaction and creating significant financial risk.

Objective:
Identify key drivers of delivery delays and provide actionable recommendations to reduce late deliveries by ≥10% within 6 months.

Tools:
Python, Tableau, Google Slides

What I Did:

  • Analyzed supply chain data (orders, shipping, delivery performance)
  • Measured late delivery rate across shipping modes and regions
  • Compared SLA (promised) vs actual delivery performance
  • Quantified financial impact using Profit at Risk
  • Built interactive Tableau dashboard for stakeholder insights

Key Insights:

  • 57.3% of deliveries were late → systemic operational issue
  • Premium shipping modes (First & Second Class) had the highest delay rates due to unrealistic SLA commitments
  • Standard Class contributed the highest financial risk (~$914K) due to large order volume
  • Delays were consistent across regions and product categories → operational issue, not demand-driven

Business Impact:

  • ~$914K profit at risk from late deliveries
  • Increased risk of customer dissatisfaction and SLA failure
  • Operational inefficiencies in fulfillment and capacity planning

Recommendations:

  • Redesign SLA for premium shipping modes to match operational capacity
  • Prioritize Standard Class improvements for maximum financial impact
  • Optimize warehouse and dispatch processes
  • Implement performance monitoring to reduce delays by ≥10% within 6 months

Retail Profitability Analysis (Superstore)
Profit decline driven by discounting & loss-making products (Tables)

Business Problem:
Despite strong sales performance, profitability remains inconsistent due to hidden losses across products and pricing strategies.

Objective:
Identify key drivers of profit loss and provide actionable strategies to improve overall profitability.

Tools:
Python, Tableau

What I Did:

  • Cleaned and analyzed retail transaction data (sales, profit, discount, customer segments)
  • Evaluated profitability across product categories, regions, and customer segments
  • Analyzed the relationship between discount levels and profit performance
  • Built regression model to validate key drivers of profitability
  • Developed Tableau dashboard to communicate insights effectively

Key Insights:

  • Profit declines are primarily driven by excessive discounting
  • Profit turns negative beyond ~20% discount threshold
  • Furniture category, especially Tables, consistently generates losses despite high sales
  • Consumer segment drives volume but operates with lower profit efficiency
  • Regional differences exist but are not a significant driver of profitability (validated through regression)

Business Impact:

  • Significant profit leakage caused by over-discounting
  • Hidden losses in high-volume product categories
  • Inefficient growth driven by low-margin customer segments

Recommendations:

  • Limit discount levels to below ~20% to protect margins
  • Re-evaluate pricing strategy for loss-making products (Tables & Furniture)
  • Shift focus toward higher-margin segments (e.g., Home Office)
  • Improve margin efficiency in high-volume segments (Consumer)
