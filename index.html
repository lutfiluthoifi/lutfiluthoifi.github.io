Delivery Delay Risk & Profitability Analysis
57.3% deliveries late → ~$914K profit at risk driven by SLA mismatch

Business Problem:
High late delivery rate (57.3%) impacting customer satisfaction and creating significant financial risk.

Objective:
Identify key drivers of delivery delays and provide actionable recommendations to reduce late deliveries by ≥10% within 6 months.

Tools & Skills:
SQL, Python, Tableau, Data Cleaning, Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), Data Storytelling

What I Did:

  • Analyzed supply chain data (orders, shipping, delivery performance)
  • Measured late delivery rate across shipping modes and regions
  • Compared SLA (promised) vs actual delivery performance
  • Quantified financial impact using Profit at Risk
  • Built interactive Tableau dashboard for stakeholder insights

Key Insights:

  • 57.3% of deliveries were late → systemic operational issue
  • Premium shipping modes (First & Second Class) had the highest delay rates due to unrealistic SLA commitments
  • Standard Class contributed the highest financial risk (~$914K) due to large order volume
  • Delays were consistent across regions and product categories → operational issue, not demand-driven

Business Impact:

  • ~$914K profit at risk from late deliveries
  • Increased risk of customer dissatisfaction and SLA failure
  • Operational inefficiencies in fulfillment and capacity planning

Recommendations:

  • Redesign SLA for premium shipping modes to match operational capacity
  • Prioritize Standard Class improvements for maximum financial impact
  • Optimize warehouse and dispatch processes
  • Implement performance monitoring to reduce delays by ≥10% within 6 months
