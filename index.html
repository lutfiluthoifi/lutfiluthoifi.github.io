Delivery Delay Risk & Profitability Analysis
57.3% deliveries late → ~$914K profit at risk driven by SLA mismatch

Business Problem:
High late delivery rate (57.3%) impacting customer satisfaction and creating significant financial risk.

Objective:
Identify key drivers of delivery delays and provide actionable recommendations to reduce late deliveries by ≥10% within 6 months.

Tools:
Python, Tableau, Google Slides

What I Did:

  • Analyzed supply chain data (orders, shipping, delivery performance)
  • Measured late delivery rate across shipping modes and regions
  • Compared SLA (promised) vs actual delivery performance
  • Quantified financial impact using Profit at Risk
  • Built interactive Tableau dashboard for stakeholder insights

Key Insights:

  • 57.3% of deliveries were late → systemic operational issue
  • Premium shipping modes (First & Second Class) had the highest delay rates due to unrealistic SLA commitments
  • Standard Class contributed the highest financial risk (~$914K) due to large order volume
  • Delays were consistent across regions and product categories → operational issue, not demand-driven

Business Impact:

  • ~$914K profit at risk from late deliveries
  • Increased risk of customer dissatisfaction and SLA failure
  • Operational inefficiencies in fulfillment and capacity planning

Recommendations:

  • Redesign SLA for premium shipping modes to match operational capacity
  • Prioritize Standard Class improvements for maximum financial impact
  • Optimize warehouse and dispatch processes
  • Implement performance monitoring to reduce delays by ≥10% within 6 months

Superstore Profitability Turnaround: Discount Strategy & RFM Segmentation
Executive dashboard simulating discount caps & rescuing 'At Risk' VIP customers.

The Business Problem:
Superstore generates a substantial volume of Sales, yet preliminary Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) revealed inconsistent profitability across product lines. While top-line revenue appears healthy, uncontrolled discounting strategies have created hidden "profit leaks" that actively drain the company's overall earnings.

The Objective:
To diagnose the root causes of financial losses at both the product and customer levels, and to design an interactive executive dashboard that enables management to simulate new pricing policies and restore profitability.

Tech Stack:
Python (RFM Modeling), Tableau (Geospatial Mapping, What-If Parameters, UI/UX Design)

The Process (What I Did):

  • Data Engineering (Python): Preprocessed historical transaction data and performed feature engineering to structure the dataset for deep-dive analysis, intentionally retaining business outliers (e.g., extreme discounts) to accurately capture profit leaks.
  • Customer Segmentation (Python): Built an RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) model to cluster the customer base into critical business segments (e.g., Champions, At Risk Big Spenders).
  • Interactive Simulation (Tableau): Developed a Dark Mode executive dashboard featuring a What-If Parameter to simulate the financial impact of capping discounts.
  • UI/UX Optimization: Implemented Viz-in-Tooltip geospatial mapping to maintain a clean, high data-ink ratio and minimize cognitive load.

Key Insights & Recommendations:

  • The Profit Killers: The Tables product category is the primary driver of financial loss, largely due to an aggressive discount strategy averaging above 20%.
  • Optimal Discount Policy: The What-If Simulation revealed that enforcing a strict 20% maximum discount cap can successfully transition historically unprofitable product lines back to breakeven or profitability.
  • Rescuing VIP Clients: Segmentation analysis identified a critical group of At Risk (Big Spenders) who are on the verge of churning. Blanket discount budgets should be strategically reallocated into targeted Win-Back Campaigns exclusively for this high-value cohort.

Projected Business Impact:

  • Profit Recovery: By simulating a 20% discount cap, the company is projected to stop 100% of profit leaks within the Tables category, significantly stabilizing the overall Profit Margin.
  • Marketing Budget Efficiency: Pivoting from a "mass product discount" strategy to a "targeted customer discount" approach allows the business to retain high-value customers without requiring additional marketing expenditures.
  • Decision-Making Acceleration: The centralized, clean UI of the dashboard transforms static reporting into a dynamic, real-time strategy simulator.
